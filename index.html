CoreDiffuse: iOS app for native generation of images with Stable Diffusion, optimized using CoreML

In this project, I investigated the capabilities of running a stable-diffusion ML model locally on my iPhone. I leveraged the power of the Apple Neural Engine for accelerating models, to deploy an app in SwiftUI capable of running completely locally on an iPhone. I also had a ton of fun building my first ever iOS app and learning Swift development, as well as learning more about the apple CoreML architecture, and diffusion models.

Image generation takes only around 15s for 30 iterations, running with the palettized UNet architecture.

Current implementation support the stable diffusion V1.5 and stable diffusion V2.1 models.

Design of 4-wheel drive system for UBC AgroBot

In this project, I designed and machined a 4-wheel drive systems for UBC AgroBots's autonomous weed-exterminating robot. The project began with idea generation, leading to the design and CAD of the wheel's full assembly. I used Onshape to create a full 3D model of the wheel assembly, with each individual part spec'd out on McMaster. The design considered a chain-drive system to provide the necessary torque to the wheels while keeping the system away from the debris from the muddy ground. With the design finalized, I machined everything by hand from scratch using the lathe, milling machine, and water jet.
